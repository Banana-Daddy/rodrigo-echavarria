Origen

A path
from clay.

Born in Colombia in 1996, the game arrived through the dirt — the slow surface that doesn’t forgive impatience. Right-handed. Quiet on the line. A clay-court player in the truest sense.

  1. 1996 · Colombia

    A childhood on the clay.

    The Colombian junior circuit. Tape lines, brushed dirt, the slow grind that builds patience and footwork before it builds anything else.

  2. 2018 · Tour

    Pro — ATP & ITF circuits.

    Four seasons on the international circuit. 49–44 career singles record. Career high singles ranking in 2020. Held the #1 ranking in Colombia. Worked alongside the country’s national champions.

  3. 2018 · Edison, NJ

    The teaching chair.

    Began coaching at Garden State Tennis Center while still actively competing — a program with deep junior development credentials and one of the most decorated teaching staffs in the Northeast.

  4. 2026 · Now

    Director of Tournament Training.

    Eight years in. Working with top-ranked juniors and competitive adults. Bilingual coaching in English and Spanish. Private lessons at $110 an hour.

Método

How the work
actually goes.

First lesson — we don’t hit. We watch, we talk, we identify the two things that, if fixed, would change everything. Then we build a plan around those two things.

Each session — technical work, tactical patterns, and mental sharpening. Match-specific scenarios for tournament players. Mechanics work for adults rebuilding strokes. Diagnostic feedback in English or in Spanish.

Between sessions — video review, drill assignments, and the patience that a clay-court coach insists on. The game is long. Build for that.
